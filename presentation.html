The Problem

Why keyword filters fail

🔍

Shallow Matching

A candidate who listed "Machine Learning" gets the same score whether they trained academic models or shipped ML to 10 million users.

📋

Skills List ≠ Reality

Career history often reveals deeper expertise than the skills section. "Deployed embedding-based retrieval to production" won't appear as a keyword.

🚫

Missing Disqualifiers

The JD explicitly disqualifies pure-research profiles and non-coding managers. Keyword search can't detect these patterns.

Our answer: Read the full picture — skills, career narratives, behavioral signals, platform activity — and score like a senior technical recruiter.

35%

Proficiency × tier weight × duration × log(endorsements) + assessment scores

20%

Gaussian peak at 7 yrs (σ=3) + title relevance + industry bonus

🏭 Career Depth / Production
15%

Production keywords in descriptions vs. research-only language

15%

Response rate, GitHub activity, interview completion, verification, recency

10%

LoRA/fine-tuning, LTR, HR-tech experience, OSS contributions, tier-1 edu

🚫 Disqualifier Penalties
×(1-p)

Multiplicative penalty: pure research, no AI skills, non-tech title, inactive

Formula: final_score = (Σ weight_i × signal_i) × (1 − penalty)

Design Philosophy

Why This Architecture?

🚀 No API Required — Fully Local

Deterministic, reproducible, zero cost. No rate limits, no latency from external services. Processes 100K candidates in 55 seconds on CPU alone.

🧠 Semantic Understanding

Rather than embedding the JD and using cosine similarity (which requires an API), we extract the semantics manually as a structured skill taxonomy — more interpretable and debuggable.

📖 Career Narrative Analysis

We read job description text, not just skill tags. "Deployed to 5M users" reveals more than a "Machine Learning" checkbox. 20+ production keywords, 11 research-penalty keywords scanned per role.

⚖️ Explainable Scores

Every score is decomposed into 6 named signals. A recruiter can look at any candidate and understand exactly why they ranked where they did — not a black-box embedding distance.

📡 Behavioral Signals as Quality Filter

Platform engagement (response rate, GitHub activity, interview completion) differentiates equally-skilled candidates. A candidate with 0.95 response rate > 0.05, all else equal.

💾 O(n) Memory — Min-Heap Design

The 487MB file is streamed line-by-line. A min-heap of size 100 holds only the current top candidates. Space complexity is O(100) regardless of input size.
